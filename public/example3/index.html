Example 3. Seeing things twice, the nice way.

Keeping display logic out of the Model with templates and the 'bindings' attribute.

If you've looked at the code in our previous example, you've seen that the model has a function setDisplayName on it. In fact, all of the initialize code in the model is about creating and maintaining the displayName attribute.

That's fine, but while there are times this can be a good thing, pure data models are best kept lite. To do that, we move the displayName driver out of the model an into the view by adding a native stickit binding. All of stickit's abilities are available to you if you need them. Just follow the stickit documentation. BonMot always defers to native stickit bindings; it will respect your choices.

In the code: You'll see we've cut the total number of lines by four, and improved both readability and comprehensibility.(slashes added to give you a visual hint that this is different from our last example)
